# Customer Discovery Template for Forward Deployed Engineers

A practical template for understanding customer problems before designing or building a solution.

Forward Deployed Engineers do not only write code.

They first understand:

- What the customer is trying to achieve
- Why the problem matters
- How the current process works
- Where the system is failing
- What constraints exist
- What success should look like

Good customer discovery prevents wasted engineering work.

Bad customer discovery leads to beautiful systems that solve the wrong problem.

---

## Why Customer Discovery Matters

In customer-facing engineering roles, the first request from the customer is often not the real problem.

A customer may say:

> “We need an AI chatbot.”

But the real problem may be:

> “Our support team spends 40 hours per week answering repetitive questions from enterprise customers.”

Or the customer may say:

> “We need a dashboard.”

But the real problem may be:

> “Leadership does not trust the current reporting because data is spread across five systems.”

A strong Forward Deployed Engineer learns to go deeper.

The goal is not to collect requirements blindly.

The goal is to understand the workflow, pain, constraints, risks, and expected business outcome.

---

## Customer Discovery Mindset

Before proposing a technical solution, think like this:

```text
Do I understand the real problem?
Do I understand who is affected?
Do I understand the current workflow?
Do I understand why this matters now?
