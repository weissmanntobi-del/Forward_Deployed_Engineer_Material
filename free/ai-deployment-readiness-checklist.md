# AI Deployment Readiness

AI deployment is not only about connecting an LLM API to an application.

A demo can work with five clean examples, but production is different.

In production, users ask unexpected questions. Data is messy. APIs fail. Costs increase. Latency matters. Security teams ask difficult questions. Customers expect reliability, not just impressive answers.

That is why Forward Deployed Engineers and Forward Deployed AI Engineers need to think beyond the prototype.

AI deployment readiness means checking whether an AI system is ready to be used by real users, inside real workflows, with real business consequences.

---

## Why AI Deployment Readiness Matters

Many AI projects fail because teams move too quickly from demo to production.

The demo usually proves:

- The model can generate useful output
- The user interface looks promising
- The basic workflow is possible
- The customer understands the vision

But production requires more than that.

Production requires:

- Reliable output quality
- Clear failure handling
- Good data quality
- Security and privacy controls
- Monitoring and logging
- Cost control
- Latency management
- Evaluation workflows
- Human review when needed
- Customer expectation management

A strong Forward Deployed Engineer does not ask only:

> “Does the AI feature work?”

A strong FDE asks:

> “Can this AI feature work reliably in the customer’s real environment?”

Use this checklist before deploying an AI feature to real users.

---

## 1. Problem Readiness

Before deploying AI, make sure the problem is clear.

Ask:

- What customer problem are we solving?
- Is AI actually needed for this problem?
- What does success look like?
- How will the customer measure value?
- What happens if the AI gives a wrong answer?
- Who is the end user?
- Is this workflow important enough to automate?
